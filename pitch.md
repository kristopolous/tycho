Tycho, named after the famous astronomer Tycho Brahe is a tool that does the following:
 1. Take cast list from imdb
 2. Find the cast in the original content (you must provide this) via the 12labs video api
 3. Uses these clips and the LTX api to create a short video ad for each of the stars

The problem is that many older assets such as a TV show from the 1980s may have a star that went on to become famous playing a role before they were famous. Fans of the new famous star may be interested in going through a monetization channel of engaging with this archival content but may not know it exists. The existing sizzle reels or trailers will not focus on the up-and-comning star who may not even be first-bill. This is the premise of Tycho - given a media and a photograph of an actor, it will make a short advertising stream focused around that particular actor using clips from the content.

Here's an example voice-over for a fictional actor Doug Smith on a fictionay tv show and a fictional streaming platform

"Did you know Doug Smith appeared on Season 4 of That Funny Show when he was a teenager?
(scenes from the show)

Watch him early in his career on That Funny Show, only on MediaPlus+: link to the asset"

The objective of Tycho is to get right up to the point of where a media owner can cross-reference content they own and provide copies of the content for analysis, using a standardized industry format.

Then there's a number of commercial "formulas" that determine how the content is packaged and marketing people can track metrics and convergence on each of the formulas for each of the actors with the knowledge that some formulas may work with only some talent.

This permits for maximizing on convergence for each of the talent.

The formulas have both structure and platform in mind. For instace "instagram" and "youtube" are different form factors (one being portraitt and the other being landscape). Those details are to be worked out later
